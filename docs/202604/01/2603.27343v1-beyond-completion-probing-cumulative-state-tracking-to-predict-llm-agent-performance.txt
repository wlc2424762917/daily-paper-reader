Abstract 

Task-completion rate is the standard proxy for LLM agent capability, but models with identical completion scores can differ substantially in their ability to track intermediate state. We introduce Working Memory Fidelity-Active Manipulation (WMF-AM), a calibrated no-scratchpad probe of cumulative arithmetic state tracking, and evaluate it on 20 open-weight models (0.5B–35B, 13 families) against a released deterministic 10-task agent battery. In a pre-specified, Bonferroni-corrected analysis, WMF-AM predicts agent performance with Kendall’s τ =0 .612 (p< 0.001 , 95 % CI [0 .360 , 0.814] ); exploratory partial-τ analyses suggest this signal persists after controlling for completion score and model scale. Three construct-isolation ablations ( K=1 control, non-arithmetic ceiling, yoked cancellation) support the interpretation that cumulative state tracking under load, rather than single-step arithmetic or entity tracking alone, is the primary difficulty source. K-calibration keeps the probe in a discriminative range where prior fixed-depth benchmarks become non-discriminative; generalization beyond this open-weight sample remains open. 

1 Introduction 

This paper investigates whether a controlled probe of cumulative arithmetic state tracking captures variance in downstream agent performance not reflected by completion alone. Task-completion met-rics are useful and widely applicable, but completion scores may compress variance in process-level capabilities that matter for reliable multi-step agent behavior. We call this the Completion Fallacy: the inference that completion success alone is sufficient for inferring latent process competence. Benchmarks such as HotpotQA [Yang et al., 2018], MMLU [Hendrycks et al., 2021a], and Agent-Bench [Liu et al., 2023] measure what LLMs accomplish; process-sensitive probes ask how models process information under load, a complementary evaluation lens. We introduce and empirically validate WMF-AM, a controlled probe for cumulative arithmetic state tracking under load, and situate it within a working interpretive taxonomy, the Cognitive Evaluation Framework (CEF). WMF-AM is distinguished from prior state-tracking work [Kim and Schuster, 2023, Huang et al., 2025, Xia et al., 2025] by cumulative arithmetic load manipulation, targeted ablation controls, and downstream predictive validity on a deterministic agent battery; Section 2 details the comparison. This paper makes four contributions. (1) Construct and probe. We formalize cumulative arith-metic state tracking under load as a measurable process-level capability and introduce WMF-AM, a

2 Related Work 

Completion-based evaluation and the process-quality gap. Standard benchmarks, MMLU [Hendrycks et al., 2021a], HotpotQA [Yang et al., 2018], AgentBench [Liu et al., 2023], HELM [Liang et al., 2023], measure outcome correctness without probing the reasoning process. This creates what we term the Completion Fallacy, an instance of multiple realizability [Putnam, 1967]: identical outcomes produced by categorically different processes, documented also as underspecification [D’Amour et al., 2022] and shortcut learning [Geirhos et al., 2020]. Within our 20-model sample, 30/105 pairs with |∆completion | ≤ 0.05 show |∆WMF-AM | ≥ 0.10 , illustrating that, in this sample, completion compresses process-level variance. 

Evidence of process-outcome dissociation. Recent work documents process-outcome gaps at scale. PAE [Xie et al., 2026] finds 27–78% of agent successes involve corrupted trajectories; Fragile Thoughts [Aravindan and Kejriwal, 2026] and Robust Answers Fragile Logic [Jiang et al., 2026] show correct answers produced despite broken reasoning chains; Turpin et al. [Turpin et al., 2023] demonstrate unfaithful chain-of-thought explanations. AgentProcessBench [Fan et al., 2026] provides 8,509 human-annotated steps showing step-level quality diverges from outcomes. Process supervision [Lightman et al., 2023, Uesato et al., 2022] trains on step-level labels; CEF measures process quality without requiring labelled training data. 

State-tracking and process-sensitive probes. A growing body of work probes how LLMs maintain and update internal state representations. Entity Tracking [Kim and Schuster, 2023] tests passive state retrieval (e.g., which container holds a given object after a sequence of swaps), while Latent State Persistence [Huang et al., 2025] extends to sequential manipulation of hidden integers—the closest prior paradigm to WMF-AM, though without arithmetic accumulation or downstream agent-predictive validation. Several recent benchmarks [Rezaee et al., 2025, Li et al., 2025] similarly probe object or entity state, and Zhang et al. [Zhang et al., 2024] motivate working-memory-style evaluation, but none pair ablation controls with an external criterion battery. Minerva [Xia et al., 2025] includes a Quantity State task that is structurally closest to WMF-AM, sequential additive/subtractive operations, yet its fixed depth of K=200 places all 20 of our study models at exactly 0.000, rendering the probe non-discriminative ( τ undefined). WMF-AM addresses this gap along three axes: K-calibration restoring discriminability across the 0.5B–35B range, a construct-isolation ablation suite, and predictive validity against a deterministic agent battery (head-to-head with Minerva in Section 5). 

3 WMF-AM and Its Interpretive Framework (CEF) 

CEF (Cognitive Evaluation Framework) is a design lens that organizes process-sensitive probes into four proposed dimensions: WMF (Working Memory Fidelity), MCC (Metacognitive Calibration), EMC (Episodic Memory Coherence), and CLA (Cognitive Load Adaptation), with 11 sub-dimensions. This dimensional structure is hypothesized; factor analysis requires N ≥30 (Appendix C; glossary in Table 11). 

> 2

Flagship probe: WMF-AM. WMF-AM (Active Manipulation) is a probe of cumulative arithmetic state tracking under load, inspired by working-memory span paradigms. An initial numeric state is presented, K sequential additive/subtractive operations arrive, and the model must report the final cumulative state. The pilot uses depths K ∈ { 3, 5, 7} (dissociation study) and K ∈ { 2, 4, 6, 8, 12 }

(yoked cancellation control), across three surface forms (points scoring, warehouse inventory, bank accounts). Prior entity tracking [Kim and Schuster, 2023] tests passive retrieval; WMF-AM tests sequential arithmetic transformation under load. 

Scope. WMF-AM measures difficulty with maintaining a running cumulative sum across K

operations. A K=1 control separates cumulative tracking from single-step arithmetic: 13/15 models achieve near-ceiling accuracy at K=1 (¯x=0 .957 ) versus ¯x=0 .151 at K=7 , indicating that cumulative load, not arithmetic skill per se, is the primary difficulty driver. 

Worked example ( K=3 , points form): “Alice starts with 10 points. Alice gains 5 points. Alice loses 3 points. Alice gains 7 points. What is Alice’s current score?” Correct answer: 19. Scoring: exact match (1 if the model outputs 19, 0 otherwise). A model that tracks each update cumulatively (10 → 15 → 12 → 19 ) succeeds; a model that performs only the last operation or guesses a plausible number fails. At K=7 , the chain length taxes state maintenance, producing the diagnostic depth ×accuracy interaction. 

Yoked control example ( K=3 ): “Alice starts with 10 points. Alice gains 5 points. Alice loses 5 points. Alice gains 3 points. Alice loses 3 points. Alice gains 7 points. Alice loses 7 points. What is Alice’s current score?” Correct answer: 10 (initial state). Each operation is immediately followed by its inverse, requiring identical arithmetic but no cumulative tracking. Preliminary pilots for MCC, EMC, and CLA are reported in Appendix N–O; construct-validity framework and falsification criteria are in Appendix I. 

4 Validation Study: WMF-AM Provides Initial Predictive Evidence for Agent Performance 

Variable definitions. Three scores are used throughout this section and should not be confused. 

WMF-AM Score : the probe output, mean accuracy over K ∈ { 3, 5, 7} depths, 4 random seeds, and 3 surface forms (points / inventory / accounts) for each model, all collected under each model’s Ollama default chat template (Section 3). Prompt-wrapper variation (bare / chat / CoT) is analyzed separately as a robustness check in Phase 5 (Section 4.2) and is not folded into the primary WMF-AM Score. (In Phase 5, “chat” denotes the same Ollama default chat wrapper used for the primary evaluation; “bare” strips it.) Outcome Correctness (OC) : the 100-item general-knowledge completion battery used as a general-capability baseline (Section 4, Phase 1); this is the “completion score” referred to throughout. Agent Battery Score (ABS) : the 10-task deterministic downstream criterion (Phase 6); this is the “agent score” or “downstream target” used in all predictive validity analyses. OC and WMF-AM serve as predictors; ABS is the downstream criterion. 

Statistical methodology. All rank correlations use Kendall’s τ -b (ties accounted for). Partial τ uses Somers residualization [Somers, 1962]. Bootstrap 95% CIs: 10,000 model-level and family-clustered resamples; family-clustered CI [0 .341 , 0.815] is virtually identical to model-level [0 .360 , 0.814] , no single family is load-bearing ( τ ∈ [0 .503 , 0.649] across leave-family-out runs). Pre-specified primary analyses (Bonferroni-corrected, α=0 .025 ): (1) predictive validity ( τ = 0 .612 ) and (2) template stability ( τ = 0 .524 ). Both were frozen before expansion-set collection; all other analyses are exploratory. 

Formal criterion. Let Yi ∈ [0 , 1] denote the outcome score (OC) and Zi ∈ [0 , 1] a process-sensitive measure (WMF-AM) for model i. Outcome metrics are statistically insufficient w.r.t. a downstream criterion D if partial τ (Z, D | Y ) > 0 at conventional α. This condition is benchmark-specific, not a universal claim. We report an exploratory test of this criterion in Section 4.1. 

Note on sample sizes. Different analyses use different subsets: N =20 for all predictive validity and completion-battery analyses (the full study set); N =15 for WMF-AM template stability and divergent validity (original 7 + expansion 8; before small models were added); N =7 for convergent validity only (original models with AGENT-PQ and CONV-RFPOC data collected). Twenty open-weight models from thirteen architectural families form the study set: seven orig-inal models (Qwen 7B/14B/32B, Llama 3.1:8b, Gemma 2:27b, DeepSeek-R1:14b, Mistral:7b), eight expansion models (Phi-3:14b, Gemma-2:9b, Qwen-2.5:3b, Llama-3.2:3b, DeepSeek-R1:7b, Mixtral:8x7b, Command-R:35b, Yi:34b), and five small baseline models (0.5B–2B parameters; Table 3). Models were evaluated in six phases. Phase 1 (Outcome Correctness) used a 100-item battery spanning five domains (factual, math, science, reasoning, language) at five difficulty levels, tempera-ture = 0 . Phase 2 (WMF-AM) administered 15 probes at depths K ∈ { 3, 5, 7} with 4 independent random seeds for all 20 models. Phase 3 (MCC-MA) applied a 20-problem self-monitoring probe. Phase 4 (Yoked Cancellation Control) administered 20 trials per depth at K ∈ { 2, 4, 6, 8, 12 } using operations that cancel (gain then lose, transfer then reverse), so the correct answer equals the initial state despite requiring identical arithmetic parsing as WMF-AM. Phase 5 (Template Harmoniza-tion) re-administered WMF-AM under three prompt wrappers (bare, chat, chain-of-thought) to test prompt-format sensitivity. Phase 6 (Agent Validation) administered 10 multi-step agent tasks (tool use, multi-step reasoning, state tracking) to all 20 models to test predictive validity. All models are Ollama-hosted open-weight variants using default chat templates, fp16 quantization where available, and greedy decoding (temperature = 0 , no top-p sampling). No task overlap exists between phases (full study matrix in Appendix K). Table 2 summarizes the experimental design.

Phase 1 uses a custom 100-item battery (five domains × five difficulty levels; score resolution 0.01) as a within-study general-capability control, not a standard benchmark suite. An exploratory log( parameter count ) control ( τ = 0 .503 , p < 0.01 ) provides a complementary proxy independent of battery choice; the joint partial τ (0.389 , p = 0 .016 ) controls for both simultaneously. With the addition of 5 small models (0.5B–2B), OC ranges from 0.44 to 0.92 (SD 

= 0 .131 , N =20 ) and correlates with agent performance ( τ = 0 .428 , p = 0 .012 ); WMF-AM shows an exploratory signal of incremental prediction (partial τ = 0 .411 , p = 0 .011 ). Among the 15 mid-to-large models ( ≥3B), OC remains compressed (SD = 0 .052 ), consistent with range restriction being a property of the model population rather than the battery design. 

Exploratory architectural observations (Appendix U). Only deepseek-r1:14b shows positive WMF −Yoked residual ( +0 .173 ), suggesting genuine cumulative tracking; all other 14 models show negative residuals, with the largest gaps in smaller models. Figure 2 shows depth-dependent degradation curves ( N =15 ). 

Alternative explanations and template sensitivity. WMF-AM scores may reflect nuisance fac-tors beyond state tracking: instruction-following ability, tokenizer effects on numeric parsing, scratchpad/chain-of-thought policy differences, or prompt-format sensitivity. Phase 5 (template harmonization) directly tests the last concern: across 15 models × 3 templates, model rankings under bare and chat wrappers are significantly correlated ( τbare,chat = 0 .524 , p = 0 .006 ), indicating that WMF-AM rankings are moderately robust to prompt-format variation. Chain-of-thought (CoT) templates push nearly all models to ceiling ( ≥ 0.93 ), eliminating variance. 

Construct boundary. WMF-AM probes internalized state tracking under the no-scratchpad regime; the CoT ceiling ( ≥ 0.93 ) is a measurement boundary, not a validity threat, relevant to deployment contexts where intermediate outputs are unmonitored [Nye et al., 2021]. Predictive validity is established within this regime; generalization to CoT-enabled or tool-augmented settings is untested. 5 Limitations, Alternative Views, and Discussion 

Comparison to Minerva and K-calibration. Minerva [Xia et al., 2025] includes a Quantity State task at K=200 ; all 20 study models score exactly 0.000, rendering τ undefined. WMF-AM’s 

K=3 /5/7 calibration targets the discriminative window (scores span [0 .050 , 0.983] ; τ =0 .619 at 

K=3 , 0.509 at K=7 , undefined at K=200 ; Appendix B). Beyond K-calibration, WMF-AM adds a construct-isolation ablation suite. 

Key limitations. Limitations are organized by severity into four buckets. 

(L1) External validity (primary limitation). The sample comprises 20 open-weight models (0.5B–35B, greedy decoding, Ollama variants); claims do not extend to closed API models, fine-tuned variants, or non-English tasks. A single held-out check (llama3.1:70b; τ =0 .627 , N =21 ) is consistent with but does not constitute replication. The agent battery uses a single seed; multi-seed ABS reliability is not established. Next step: extend to N ≥30 including API models. 

(L2) Construct validity. The K=1 control separates cumulative tracking from single-step arith-metic; CoT collapses all models to ceiling ( ≥ 0.93 ), a construct boundary. Rival explanations (instruction-following, tokenizer effects) are mitigated by cross-template stability ( τ =0 .524 ) but not eliminated (Appendix J). A parsing artifact affects two verbose models (deepseek-r1:14b/7b); 88/90 responses contain the correct result. 

(L3) Sample size and dependence structure. N =20 supports the two pre-specified primary analyses but not factor analysis; the four-dimension CEF structure is a provisional taxonomy, not a validated empirical model. Family non-independence is substantial (6 Qwen, 3 DeepSeek mod-els); within-family ordering may trivially inflate τ . The within-Qwen robustness check ( τ =0 .894 ,

p=0 .016 , N =6 ) is included but family-balanced sampling is needed for full rigor. 

(L4) Comparator scope. The completion proxy is a custom 100-item battery, not a standard benchmark (MMLU, GSM8K). Partial-τ results controlling for completion or scale are exploratory; held-out replication is needed. 

What WMF-AM is not. Ablations indicate WMF-AM is unlikely to primarily measure general arithmetic skill or working-memory capacity ( K=1 control), generic entity tracking (non-arithmetic ceiling), or arithmetic parsing (yoked τ ≈ 0). Instruction-following and tokenizer effects are mitigated by cross-template stability but not eliminated; the mechanism linking cumulative tracking difficulty to diverse agent tasks is not established. 

Scope of contribution. WMF-AM is validated in one regime: open-weight models (0.5B–35B), greedy decoding, no scratchpad. The two pre-specified findings ( τ =0 .612 ; τ =0 .524 ) are in-sample evidence for this slice of model space; they do not generalize to process-sensitive probes at large. The CEF taxonomy is an organizing agenda, not an empirical result. 

Implications. Evaluation suites would benefit from process probes alongside completion: a load-varied probe [Zhang et al., 2024], a metacognitive component [Lightman et al., 2023], and a difficulty gradient. Next steps: extend to API models ( N ≥30 ), test against standard benchmarks, and replicate across probe families. 

Reproducibility. The 10-task agent battery, all WMF-AM probe templates, scoring scripts, per-model raw outputs, and the pre-registered analysis plan are available at https://github.com/ dengzhe-hou/CEF-WMF-AM .

A Full Motivation and Related Work 

Three failure modes of completion-only evaluation. (F1) Diagnostic blindness : models with near-identical completion scores can have very different process profiles. (F2) Uninformative failure diagnosis : current benchmarks report that an agent failed, not why, whether the bottleneck is state tracking, metacognitive calibration, or episodic confusion. (F3) Safety blindness : pattern-matching agents (which fail predictably at distribution shift) are indistinguishable from genuine reasoners on standard benchmarks. 

Empirical support. Multiple realizability is empirically documented at scale. Fragile Thoughts [Ar-avindan and Kejriwal, 2026] and Robust Answers Fragile Logic [Jiang et al., 2026] show correct answers produced despite corrupted reasoning. Turpin et al. [Turpin et al., 2023] and Lanham et al. [Lanham et al., 2023] show CoT can be systematically unfaithful. PAE [Xie et al., 2026]: 27– 78% of successful completions involve corrupted intermediate actions. AgentProcessBench [Fan et al., 2026]: 8,509-step human annotation shows weaker models inflate correct-step ratios via early termination. Chaduvula et al. [Chaduvula et al., 2026]: state-tracking inconsistency is 2.7× more prevalent in failed agent runs (TAU-bench). 

Positioning relative to prior work. Entity Tracking [Kim and Schuster, 2023] is prior art for WMF-AM’s design; Latent State Persistence [Huang et al., 2025] probes sequential latent-state manipulation on hidden integers, a closely related paradigm. Minerva [Xia et al., 2025]: see Table 5. CEF adapts cognitive science measurement logic [Miller, 1956, Baddeley and Hitch, 1974, Flavell, 1979, Nelson and Narens, 1990, Tulving et al., 1972, Sweller, 1988] for API-level LLM evaluation without claiming LLMs have human cognition. Process supervision [Lightman et al., 2023, Uesato et al., 2022] trains models using step-level labels; CEF measures process quality. Mechanistic interpretability explains why a score is low; CEF identifies that it is low.

Each dimension has a cognitive science anchor, a documented evaluation gap, and API-level testability. Labels like “working memory” are operational shorthand, not mechanistic claims (see Appendix G). Construct validity framework. Following Cronbach and Meehl [1955], Messick [1989], Campbell and Fiske [1959], and Jacobs and Wallach [2021], each dimension requires convergent validity (correlation with independent process-sensitive measures) and divergent validity (low correlation with completion). CEF would be falsified if all dimensions collapsed onto a single factor indistinguishable from task completion. WMF-IR (Interference Resistance, Tier 2). Adapts the List-A/List-B proactive interference paradigm. Currently shows ceiling ( = 1 .0 all models); needs redesign.

L Yoked Cancellation Control Details 

The yoked cancellation control uses operation pairs that cancel: “Alice gains 3 points” followed immediately by “Alice loses 3 points.” The prompt format, entity names, and depth structure match WMF-AM exactly, so the arithmetic parsing demand is identical. The correct answer equals the initial state, but unlike the original inert control, the model must process each operation to verify cancellation. This eliminates the trivial shortcut of ignoring operations.

Q Full Validity Analyses 

Below we provide additional details on the validity analyses summarized in Section 4.5. 

Convergent validity details. AGENT-PQ: 10 multi-step scenarios scored by LLM judge (GPT-4o) on 4 rubric dimensions. CONV-RFPOC: 15 reasoning problems with counterfactual chain perturbation. RFPOC = 1 − P (same answer | perturbed chain ). Full protocol in Appendix R. Limitation: LLM judge may introduce shared bias. 

Construct separation (WMF-AM ↔ CLA-DC). τ = 0 .905 : no evidence of separability at N =7 .Both involve state tracking under load. Planned discriminant test at N ≥ 15 . CLA-CR-v3 provides a structurally independent CLA measure. Even if CLA-DC merges with WMF-AM, WMF-AM, MCC-MA, and EMC-lite remain separable ( τ ≤ 0.250 ). 

Sensitivity. LOMO for CONV-RFPOC vs AGENT-PQ: τ ranges 0.600–1.000 (mean 0.833). Leave-one-family-out (Qwen, N =4 ): τ = 0 .667 . After Holm correction, RFPOC–AGENT-PQ remains significant ( padj = 0 .021 ). 

Multiple testing. Two primary hypotheses; all others exploratory. 

Benchmark governance. All headline results use frozen protocol versions. Probe revisions were motivated by floor/ceiling effects, not model-specific tuning. 

R AGENT-PQ Evaluation Protocol 

AGENT-PQ uses 10 multi-step scenarios where each model operates within a tool-use scaffold. A separate LLM judge (GPT-4o, temperature = 0 ) scores each trajectory on four rubric dimensions: plan coherence, state tracking, error recovery, goal adherence. AGENT-PQ = mean of 4 rubric scores /4.AGENT-TC is the binary success rate on the same scenarios. 

CONV-RFPOC administers 15 reasoning problems with counterfactual chain perturbation. RF-POC = 1 − P (same answer | perturbed chain ).

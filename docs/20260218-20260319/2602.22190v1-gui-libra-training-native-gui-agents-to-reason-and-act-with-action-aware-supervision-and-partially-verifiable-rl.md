## 摘要
开源原生 GUI 智能体在长程导航任务上仍落后于闭源系统。这一差距源于两个局限性：高质量、动作对齐的推理数据短缺，以及直接采用通用后训练流水线而忽视了 GUI 智能体面临的独特挑战。我们在这些流水线中发现了两个根本性问题：(i) 带有思维链 (CoT) 推理的标准监督微调 (SFT) 往往会损害定位 (grounding) 能力；(ii) 逐步 RLVR 式训练面临部分可验证性问题，即多个动作可能都是正确的，但仅使用单个演示动作进行验证。这使得离线逐步指标在预测在线任务成功率方面表现乏力。在这项工作中，我们提出了 GUI-Libra，这是一套针对这些挑战量身定制的训练方案。首先，为了缓解动作对齐推理数据的稀缺，我们引入了数据构建和过滤流水线，并发布了一个精选的 81K GUI 推理数据集。其次，为了协调推理与定位，我们提出了动作感知 SFT，它混合了“先推理后行动”和“直接行动”的数据，并对 token 进行重新加权以强调动作和定位。第三，为了在部分可验证性下稳定强化学习，我们指出了 RLVR 中被忽视的 KL 正则化的重要性，并证明 KL 置信域对于提高离线到在线的可预测性至关重要；我们还引入了成功自适应缩放，以降低不可靠负梯度的权重。在各种网页和移动端基准测试中，GUI-Libra 一致地提高了逐步准确率和端到端任务完成率。我们的结果表明，精心设计的后训练和数据策划可以在无需昂贵的在线数据收集的情况下，释放出显著更强的任务解决能力。我们发布了数据集、代码和模型，以促进对具备推理能力的 GUI 智能体进行高效数据后训练的进一步研究。

## Abstract
Open-source native GUI agents still lag behind closed-source systems on long-horizon navigation tasks. This gap stems from two limitations: a shortage of high-quality, action-aligned reasoning data, and the direct adoption of generic post-training pipelines that overlook the unique challenges of GUI agents. We identify two fundamental issues in these pipelines: (i) standard SFT with CoT reasoning often hurts grounding, and (ii) step-wise RLVR-tyle training faces partial verifiability, where multiple actions can be correct but only a single demonstrated action is used for verification. This makes offline step-wise metrics weak predictors of online task success. In this work, we present GUI-Libra, a tailored training recipe that addresses these challenges. First, to mitigate the scarcity of action-aligned reasoning data, we introduce a data construction and filtering pipeline and release a curated 81K GUI reasoning dataset. Second, to reconcile reasoning with grounding, we propose action-aware SFT that mixes reasoning-then-action and direct-action data and reweights tokens to emphasize action and grounding. Third, to stabilize RL under partial verifiability, we identify the overlooked importance of KL regularization in RLVR and show that a KL trust region is critical for improving offline-to-online predictability; we further introduce success-adaptive scaling to downweight unreliable negative gradients. Across diverse web and mobile benchmarks, GUI-Libra consistently improves both step-wise accuracy and end-to-end task completion. Our results suggest that carefully designed post-training and data curation can unlock significantly stronger task-solving capabilities without costly online data collection. We release our dataset, code, and models to facilitate further research on data-efficient post-training for reasoning-capable GUI agents.
